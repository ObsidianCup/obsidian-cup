The story

Our Story

Obsidian Cup is a try. A genuine, wholehearted try at giving sport in Bangladesh the experience it actually deserves. Better venues, real production, and a standard of care that players can feel from the moment they step onto the court or the pitch.

Behind it is Uriana, with Tafhim holding things down on the field, and a founding circle, Masheer, Nabid, and Rifa, who are always there when it counts. No corporate backing, no big team. Just people who love the game and turn up every time.

We want this magic to spread. The feeling of a well-run tournament, of a crowd that's alive, of walking off that court knowing the experience was worth everything. That feeling belongs to everyone. And we're here to make sure more people get to feel it.

"This isn't just a tournament. This is our air. We breathe it, we build it, we give it to everyone who steps onto that court. Because through sport, we find ourselves. We find each other. And we find something worth fighting for."

On this court we build lifelong friendships, real memories, and something that matters far beyond the final score.

Where this is going

The Vision

"We started with one cause. We plan to carry many more."

Volleyball was the beginning. Football is next. But underground sport in Bangladesh deserves a real home across the board — not just one court, not just one game. We want to be that home, for every sport, every player, every person who showed up and felt like they didn't belong anywhere else.

The next chapter

A Stage for Every Flame

We are working toward building a proper platform for our athletes. A space where those who burn with passion get the chance to show it. Where dedication is seen, where effort is recognised, where fire meets the audience it deserves.

In Bangladesh, most athletes never get that moment. They train, they compete, they give everything — and still go unrecognised. The system wasn't built for them. We want to change that, slowly, intentionally, and with real care. Starting with the courts we already know, and building outward from there.

"Every athlete deserves a stage. We're building it."
