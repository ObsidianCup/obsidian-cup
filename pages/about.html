The story

Our Story

Obsidian Cup is a try. A genuine, wholehearted try at giving sports in Bangladesh the experience it actually deserves. Better venues, real production, and a standard of care that players can feel from the moment they step onto the field or court.

Behind it is Uriana, Tafhim, Masheer, Rifa, Nabid, Nazia, and Talha: a small circle that keeps showing up for the game, the players, and the details that make a tournament feel cared for.

We want this magic to spread. The feeling of a well-run tournament, of a crowd that's alive, of walking off knowing the experience was worth everything. That feeling belongs to everyone. Football has now joined the story, volleyball keeps growing, and every season teaches us how to build the next one with more care.

"This isn't just a tournament. This is our air. We breathe it, we build it, we give it to everyone who steps on that court. Because through sport, we find ourselves. We find each other. And we find something worth fighting for."

On this court we build lifelong friendships, real memories, and something that matters far beyond the final score.

Where this is going

The Vision

"We started with one cause. We plan to carry many more."

Volleyball was the beginning. But sport in Bangladesh deserves a real home across the board, not just one court. We want to help build that home for every sport, every player, and every person who shows up ready to belong.

The next chapter

A Stage for Every Flame

We are working toward building a proper platform for our athletes. A space where those who burn with passion get the chance to show it. Where dedication is seen, where effort is recognised, where fire meets the audience it deserves.

In Bangladesh, most athletes never get that moment. They train, they compete, they give everything — and still go unrecognised. The system wasn't built for them. We want to change that, slowly, intentionally, and with real care. Starting with the courts we already know, and building outward from there.

"Every athlete deserves a stage. We're building it."
